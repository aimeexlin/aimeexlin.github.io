TECHNOLOGY TO ADVANCE TREATMENT OF CHILDREN WITH MOVEMENT DISORDERS

This paired project was completed for my Honours degree. I created scaled and subject-specific models of the lower limb of a typically developed child and child with cerebral palsy to compare the estimated structure and function of the models using Python and OpenSim. I found that the differences in structure lead to differences in the estimated joint kinematics and muscle forces, highlighting the importance of having accurate geometries in musculoskeletal models. The methods established can also be used to investigate relationships between the accuracy of modelling methods and the severity of a child's cerebral palsy.

BIOMECHANICAL AND ERGONOMIC ANALYSIS OF JOINT MOBILISATION TASKS IN PHYSICAL THERAPY

This project was a practicum project in exercise science, taken as an elective. I investigated whether performing joint mobilisation tasks increased the risk of work-related musculoskeletal disorders (WMSDs) for physiotherapists. Analyses were carried out on motion capture data using existing software to find RULA and REBA scores, low back compression forces, and joint strength capabilities at the most awkward posture for each task and subject. Two-way mixed ANOVA was performed using R for each dependent variable. All three tasks investigated can increase the risk of WMSDs for physiotherapists.

REPRESENTING A BIOLOGICAL SYSTEM

This project was completed with four other students for a bioinstrumentation course. We created a system that replicates the distension of the frog vocal sac. We modelled the system using a balloon and investigated the relationships between pressure, temperature, volume, flow impedance and stress-strain as it was inflated and deflated. I analysed the volume of the balloon in LabVIEw using a video recording of it being inflated and deflated. The video was recorded by another group member who had set up the experiment and recorded the pressure and temperature data simultaneously.

FRAMEWORK TO CAPTURE AND ANALYSE DEXTEROUS OBJECT MANIPULATION

This project was completed for a Summer Research Scholarship at the Auckland Bioengineering Institute over a period of 10 weeks. I used SOLIDWORKS to design a set of standard manipulation objects capable of encasing a Shimmer3 wireless multi-sensor unit. Two sets were constructed, one using Ecoflex 00-30 silicone and the other using 3D printed PLA plastic. I also imported the Shimmer3 data into MATLAB to visualise the movement of the standard manipulation objects and was able to estimate object orientations in near real-time.

COMBINING IMAGES

This project was completed for an engineering computation and software development course. I wrote MATLAB code that creates two different kinds of images from an arbitrary sequence of images that feature someone (or something) moving through a static scene. These images could be from a series of photos or a selection of frames taken from a movie. One kind of image was an action shot, a single image that features the moving person or object shown in different locations. The other kind of image was one where the action has been removed, eliminating the moving person (or object) from the image, leaving only the static scene visible.
