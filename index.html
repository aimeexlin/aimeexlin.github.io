TECHNOLOGY TO ADVANCE TREATMENT OF CHILDREN WITH MOVEMENT DISORDERS

This paired project was completed for my Honours degree. I created scaled and subject-specific models of the lower limb of a typically developed child and child with cerebral palsy to compare the estimated structure and function of the models using Python and OpenSim. I found that the differences in structure lead to differences in the estimated joint kinematics and muscle forces, highlighting the importance of using accurate geometries in musculoskeletal models. The methods established can also be used to investigate relationships between the accuracy of modelling methods and the severity of a child's cerebral palsy.

HUMAN ACTIVITY RECOGNITION

This project was completed for a signal processing course taken as an elective. I recorded linear accelerations from my phone during walking and running and used the Python libraries tsfresh and scikit-learn to extract relevant features to create a time-series classification model.

BIOMECHANICAL AND ERGONOMIC ANALYSIS OF JOINT MOBILISATION TASKS IN PHYSICAL THERAPY

This project was a practicum project in exercise science, taken as an elective. I investigated whether performing joint mobilisation tasks increased the risk of work-related musculoskeletal disorders (WMSDs) for physiotherapists. I analysed motion capture data using existing software to find RULA and REBA scores, low back compression forces, and joint strength capabilities at the most awkward posture for each task and subject. I then used R to perform two-way mixed ANOVA for each dependent variable. All three tasks investigated can increase the risk of WMSDs for male and female physiotherapists.

REPRESENTING A BIOLOGICAL SYSTEM

This project was completed in a group of five students. We created a system that replicates the distension of a frog vocal sac. We modelled the system using a balloon and investigated the relationships between pressure, temperature, volume, flow impedance and stress-strain as it was inflated and deflated. I analysed the volume of the balloon in LabVIEW using a video recorded by another group member who had set up the experiment and also recorded pressure and temperature data simultaneously.

FRAMEWORK TO CAPTURE AND ANALYSE DEXTEROUS OBJECT MANIPULATION

This project was completed for a Summer Research Scholarship at the Auckland Bioengineering Institute over a period of 10 weeks. I used SOLIDWORKS to design a set of standard manipulation objects capable of encasing a Shimmer3 wireless multi-sensor unit and constructed two sets, one using Ecoflex 00-30 silicone and the other using 3D printed PLA plastic. I imported the Shimmer3 data into MATLAB to visualise the movement of the standard manipulation objects and was able to estimate object orientations in near real-time.

HANDHELD COLOUR METER: THE COLOURSTICK

This project was completed in a group of four students. We designed the case, circuitry, and software to build a handheld colour measurement device. The device operated by measuring the reflectances of red, green, and blue LEDs using a photodiode. I contributed to the design of the circuitry and software using Altium Designer and LabVIEW, and testing with a breadboard and the assembled device.
